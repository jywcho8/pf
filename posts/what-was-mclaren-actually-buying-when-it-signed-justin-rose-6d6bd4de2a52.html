What Was McLaren Actually Buying When It Signed Justin Rose?

2026-07-23 · Read on Medium ↗

When McLaren (the Formula 1 team) launched a golf division in 2026 and unveiled Justin Rose as its first PGA Tour player and an investor in the business, the reaction split in two. Purists called it a vanity project, and marketers called it genius. But the more useful question is: what, exactly, was McLaren buying?

Golf has two different sponsorship markets, and they pay for two different things.

  1. Equipment brands pay for skill. Their whole pitch is that the gear works, and nothing proves that like a good player winning with it.
  2. Non-endemic brands (i.g. banks, watches, airlines, car companies, etc.) pay for attention. The logos on the shirt and bag that have nothing to do with striking a golf ball. All they want is their name in front of as many eyes as possible.

One market pays for what you do. The other pays for who’s watching. Justin Rose is interesting because his deal has a foot in each.

Rose the iron player vs. Rose the attention magnet

Rose’s iron play, long his signature, ranks around the 70th percentile of the tour. This is above the bar where equipment brand usually shop, but well short of the genuine elite, who live at the 95th and up. His public visibility is a different story. In 2025 his Wikipedia traffic ranked in the 99th percentile and it had roughly quadrupled in a single year.

Why? In April 2025, he stood on the closing holes at Augusta in a playoff for the green jacket. This is the one that completed Rory McIlroy’s career Grand Slam and became one of the most-watched moments in golf that year. Rose lost the playoff and won the internet.

The timing: McLaren chose Rose before the spike

Here is the twist. McLaren announced Rose in April 2026. However, this is the reveal, not the decision. By Rose’s own account, he had been inside the engineering process “well over a year” before the reveal. They were testing the very first editions of the club at McLaren’s Woking campus. This is about 20 minutes from Rose’s house.

Work backward from “well over a year,” the ambassador selection lands around late 2024 to early 2025. This is before the Masters playoff that quadrupled his visibility. In other words, McLaren did not pick Rose because he had become more visible. They had already chosen him when he was a mid-tier visibility veteran.English heritage, geographic access, iron-player credibility, availability (he had been equipment-free since leaving Honma in 2020), and a willingness to co-develop the product and take equity.

What the attention market did govern was the timing of the announcement. McLaren held the reveal until his visibility sat at an all-time high and paired it with the F1 Miami Grand Prix. This is owning a substantive partnership early and releasing it at the single best moment to be seen.

So, what did McLaren Golf actually buy?

Both, in order. The substance of the deal reads like the equipment market: a credible iron player, co-developing real clubs, with an ownership stake that aligns him far beyond a logo. The packaging reads like the attention market: a marquee, elegant, major-champion name, revealed at peak visibility under a global-ambassador banner. McLaren Golf is filed as an equipment brand, and was built like one. However, it was launched like a watch brand.

Was it a smart signing? Yes.

A start-up cannot just sign the best iron player in the world. Those players are locked into companies that can outspend a first-year brand many times over.

A start-up’s real shortlist is the narrow intersection of credible and visible with available and willing to co-invest. Rose, a former World №1, a US Open champion and Olympic gold medalist, equipment-free, high-attention, and prepared to help build and own the product, lands almost dead-center in it. For a launch, close to optimal. As a long-term performance anchor, the case is shakier. He’s 45, his driving has slipped, production delays pushed his on-course debut to mid-season, and his opening rounds with the new clubs were rough. This was a partnership built on substance but rolled out for maximum noise, not a bet that the irons would top leaderboards next season.

Who might McLaren sign next?

If the logic above is right, it also tell you who is next. Run the same two-axis screen over the 2025 field, but filter to players who are not locked to a major OEM.
