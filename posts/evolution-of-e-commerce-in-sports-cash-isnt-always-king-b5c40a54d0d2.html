Evolution of E-commerce in Sports — Cash Isn’t Always King

On January 15, 2025, LIV Golf partnered with Coupang Play. Coupang, often called the “Amazon of South Korea”, is renowned for its overnight shipping (Coupang e-commerce), food delivery services (Coupang Eats), and sports live streaming services (Coupang Play). This collaboration highlights how e-commerce is reshaping the sports industry by leveraging digital platforms to enhance fan engagement and revenue generation.

Coupang’s expansion into sports industry demonstrates how e-commerce platforms are pursuing new revenue streams. Having dominated the last-mile e-commerce war in Korea, Coupang is strategically targeting the sports industry to build user loyalty. Through Coupang Play, its users can access global sports content, including European Football League matches, Formula 1, and LIV Golf. Fans can now not only purchase daily commodities through the platform, but also consume sports-related products and services.

Coupang Play’s role as a partner goes beyond traditional branding; it represents a shift in how sports events are marketed and monetized. For instance, fans watching LIV Golf tournaments can set up notifications for events. With Coupang’s expertise in nudges and notifications — though at times they can be very annoying -, fans can stay updated with schedules and easily follow live sports events. I find this feature of Coupang Play’s sports events very appealing as a Formula 1 fan. The timeline of Formula 1 shifts as it moves across countries throughout the season. Coupang Play’s notifications solve this issue, unlike traditional TV channels, which I don’t have easy access to.

This partnership illustrates the idea that cash is no longer king in the modern economy; instead, digital innovation, user experience, and convenience are changing the sports-viewing experience.
