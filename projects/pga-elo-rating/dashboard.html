Which signals predict — the evidence

8 rolling seasons, matched controls, Bonferroni-corrected. Momentum (rising trend) was tested and predicts nothing — it is not shown anywhere as a signal.
To predict who improves: use the gap (Gems). To predict who contends: use the level (Elite skill). To know who to avoid: Fade risk.

Methodology & limitations
Skill Rating — within-season z-scored SG components, weighted by their regression impact on four result targets (SG Total, top-10s, log money, FedExCup rank): OTT .29 / APP .29 / PUTT .27 / ARG .15. Scale 1500 + 150σ.
Outcome Elo — one multi-player Elo update per season vs the whole field: R′ = R + 200·(S − E), between seasons R regresses 15% to 1500. Season-aggregate data means no within-season form; that is the main limitation.
Watchlist model — Ridge regression, retrained each season on prior seasons only (no look-ahead), projecting next-season top-10 count from skill, results, Elo, age. Validation across 7 graded seasons: rank ρ = 0.54; 75% of top-15 picks hit ≥4 top-10s (field base 34%).
Groups — Gems: skill ≥ +0.5σ & gap ≤ −0.5σ (trend filter dropped — momentum showed no predictive power). Elite: skill ≥ +1σ. Young: ≤27 & skill ≥ +0.3σ. Fade: gap ≥ +0.5σ.
Data: player_season_cleaned2.csv · 1,767 player-seasons · 441 players. Every number reproducible in the companion Colab notebook. Statistical reference, not betting advice.
